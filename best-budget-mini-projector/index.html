Quick answer

If you want a no-fuss mini projector at a rock-bottom price, the Vankyo Leisure 480 is the best overall budget pick for casual movie nights.

The Yaber Y30 is better suited for first-time buyers who want a slightly more polished setup experience and brighter image at a modest price premium.

Mini projectors have come a long way from fuzzy, dim novelty gadgets. Today, even budget-friendly models can throw a respectable 100-inch picture in a dark room, making them a solid alternative to a big TV for occasional movie nights, gaming, or backyard screenings. The key is knowing what you’re trading off: native resolution (most budget models are 480p or 854×480, even if they “support” 1080p input), brightness (typically 50–200 ANSI lumens), and built-in sound quality. A good budget pick delivers decent contrast, reliable build quality, and enough connectivity for streaming sticks. When choosing, focus on native resolution over “supported” numbers, check for keystone correction (it saves you from awkward table placement), and read owner feedback about fan noise and longevity. The two projectors below have been consistently praised by buyers and expert roundups for hitting the sweet spot between price and performance for casual home use.

Best for specific needs

Best for budget movie nights

If you just want a big picture for casual movie nights with minimal investment, the Vankyo Leisure 480 is your best bet. It’s cheap, reliable, and includes all the cables and a tripod. The 480p resolution is fine for animated films or older classics, but modern blockbusters with lots of fine detail will look soft on a 100-inch screen. Our pick: Vankyo Leisure 480.

Best for beginners wanting a complete kit

The Yaber Y30 is the projector to pick if you want a turnkey experience. It comes with a screen, tripod, and better built-in speakers, so you don’t need to buy anything else. The improved contrast and color make it easier to get a pleasing image right out of the box, and the remote control is well laid out. It’s a slightly higher investment than the Vankyo, but you get noticeably better quality. Our pick: Yaber Y30.

Best for dark-room gaming on a micro budget

Both projectors can handle casual gaming thanks to their HDMI ports and low input lag for the price tier, but the Yaber Y30 edges ahead with its cleaner image and built-in speaker that doesn’t require separate audio. Keep expectations in check: you’re playing at 480p, so text-heavy RPGs or competitive shooters aren’t ideal. For platformers, racing games, or retro titles in a dim room, either model will work. Our pick: Yaber Y30.

Our verdict

Both the Vankyo Leisure 480 and the Yaber Y30 represent the best value in budget mini projectors for 2026. The Vankyo is the absolute cheapest way to get a big screen at home, while the Yaber offers a noticeably smoother experience for a modest price bump. For most casual viewers, the Yaber Y30’s better audio and image quality make it worth the extra few dollars, especially if it’s your first projector. If your budget is truly rock-bottom and you don’t mind a little fan noise, the Vankyo Leisure 480 will still put a smile on your face.
